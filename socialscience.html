Suitable soil for avocado's growth

Avocado trees thrive in well-aerated and loose soil.
Limestone, sandy loam and decomposed granite are the main soil types that help in good growth of avocado.
Avocado trees can tolerate both acidic and alkaline soils, the best pH range for a successful tree ranges between 5 and 7 ph.
Avocado trees must have good soil drainage for healthy growth.
Soil with slightly dry conditions are best for the avocado.
Wet soil with a muddy texture will surely harm the tree.

Climate required for avocado

Depending on the race and varieties, avocados can thrive and perform well in climatic conditions ranging from true tropical to warmer parts of the temperate zone.
Mexican avocados stand up to the coldest winter temperatures, down to -9 degrees celsius, while West Indian types will die in temperatures below freezing, at 0 degree celsius.
It can not tolerate the hot dry winds and frosts of northern India.
Climatically, it is grown in tropical or semitropical areas experiencing some rainfall in summer, and in humid, subtropical summer rainfall areas.

Where did avocado originate

They are thought to have originated in the Tehuacan Valley in the state of Puebla, Mexico, although fossil evidence suggests similar species were much more widespread millions of years ago.
Avocado trees were first planted in Florida in 1833 and then in California in 1856.
